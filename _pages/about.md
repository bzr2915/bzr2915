Bi, Zhaori, Ph.D., is an Assistant Professor at Fudan University, Shanghai. He obtained his Master's and Ph.D. degrees at the University of Texas at Dallas in Electrical Engineering and Computer Engineering in May 2013 and December 2017, respectively. In 2017, he worked as an intern CAD engineer at the R&D division of Austria Mikro Systeme, Plano, United States. He joined Fudan University in 2018. His research focuses on fusion circuit design, circuit performance optimization, and medical AI applications. He is an Associate Editor of Integration, The VLSI Journal.

I am looking for students and collaborators who have not yet made peace with the given. In an age where certainties dissolve as fast as they form, the temptation is to let algorithms settle every question and to mistake comfort for fulfillment. I prefer to work with people who resist that stillness: those who remain genuinely curious about the world, who find pleasure in the texture of life itself, and who sense that the most important task of our time is not to replicate human intelligence but to discover what, in us, *exceeds* any replication. If you believe that the point of technology is to enlarge — not narrow — the space of human possibility, I would be glad to hear from you.
